Help & FAQ

Find answers to common questions and troubleshooting tips below.

Any YubiKey that supports HMAC-SHA1 challenge-response works with Yubi AppGate. This includes the YubiKey 5 series (5 NFC, 5C, 5C NFC, 5Ci, 5C Nano), the YubiKey 5 FIPS series, and older YubiKey 4 models. The key must have an HMAC-SHA1 secret programmed in Slot 1 or Slot 2 using YubiKey Manager (ykman) or the YubiKey Personalization Tool.
  1. Install YubiKey Manager on your computer.
  2. Insert your YubiKey and open YubiKey Manager.
  3. Navigate to Applications → OTP.
  4. Select a slot (Slot 1 or Slot 2) and choose Challenge-Response.
  5. Select HMAC-SHA1 and either generate a secret or enter your own 20-byte hex secret.
  6. Click Finish.

Then enroll the same slot in the Yubi AppGate app by tapping or connecting your YubiKey.

  • Make sure NFC is enabled in your phone's Settings.
  • Hold the YubiKey flat against the back of your phone, near the NFC antenna (usually upper-center).
  • Keep the key steady for 1–2 seconds — don't tap and pull away too quickly.
  • Remove any thick phone case that might block NFC signals.
  • Check the transport status in the app's Setup tab to confirm NFC shows "Ready."
  • Ensure your phone supports USB OTG (On-The-Go).
  • Plug the YubiKey directly into the USB-C port (not through a hub).
  • If prompted for USB permission, tap Allow.
  • Make sure CCID mode is enabled on your YubiKey (it is by default). You can verify via YubiKey Manager under Interfaces.

Troubleshooting Tips

The lock screen doesn't appear when I open a protected app

  • Verify the accessibility service is enabled (Setup → Open Accessibility Settings).
  • Some Android manufacturers aggressively kill background services. Check your phone's battery optimization settings and exclude Yubi AppGate.
  • On some devices, you may need to allow "Display over other apps" in system settings.

YubiKey error: "Failed to select OTP applet"

  • The OTP applet may be disabled on your YubiKey. Open YubiKey Manager, go to Interfaces, and ensure OTP is enabled for NFC and/or USB.

App locked itself — how do I get back in?

  • Yubi AppGate protects itself once a YubiKey is enrolled. Simply tap your enrolled YubiKey to authenticate and access the app.
  • If you've lost your YubiKey, you can clear the app's data via Settings → Apps → Yubi AppGate → Clear Data to reset enrollment. This will remove all protection settings.
